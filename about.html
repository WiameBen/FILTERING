Climatic Indices

The climatic indices used for filtering are:

FD: Number of frost days
The number of days in a period with a daily minimum temperature below 0°C

ID: Number of icing days
The number of days with the maximum temperature was less than 0°C

SU: Number of summer days
The number of days with maximum temperatures exceeding 25 °C

DTmax35: Days with Tmax more than 35
The number of days in a period with a daily maximum temperature over 35°C

TR: Number of tropical nights
The number of days when the temperature does not fall below 20 °

R5mm: Days with rain at least 5 mm
The number of days with rainfall larger than 5 mm

R10mm: Days with rain at least 10 mm
The number of days with rainfall larger than 10 mm

SDII: Simple precipitation intensity index
The sum of precipitation in wet days (days with >1mm of precipitation) and dividing that by the number of wet days in the period.

Rx5day: Maximum 5-day precipitation
The index gives the maximum precipitation amount that occurred over 5 consecutive days in a given period

PRECtot: Total precipitation
The total accumulated precipitation (in mm) from “wet days” during the period of interest (year, season or month)

DTR: Daily temperature range
The difference between the daily maximum and daily minimum air temperature.

CDD: Consecutive Dry Days
The number of consecutive days with less than 1mm of rain up to a maximum of 365 days.

CWD: Consecutive Wet Days
The largest number of consecutive “wet days” (where R ≥ 1mm) during the period of interest (year, season or month)
